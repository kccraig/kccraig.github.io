Drill Here

Soda Springs was always known for the natural springs for which it is named. It was in 1934 that they decided to create another attraction for the tourists so started drilling down into the ground to create a hotpool. Unbeknownst to the city dwellers, they were drilling into a pocket of carbonized water under high pressure. The town created the first man made geyser and after figuring out how to cap it, they started releasing it on schedule. Mission accomplished!

The best part of the springs is that you can drink it. Early settlers were happy to find the clean carbonated water was safe to drink. So, after you're finished gawking at the geyser plume fill up you're jug and enjoy that clean spring water.
