Fish Haven Idaho

Tiny but Proud

When you hear the bell, the meeting is starting. The LDS Church and accompanying bell was built in Fish Haven in 1868 just four years after the first settlers arrived. That first day of discovery Joseph C Rich and John Bagley threw a net in the Bear Lake and pulled out 96 trout. Fish Haven had a name and settlers had founded a city. Most of the original settlers left quickly but those who stayed behind built a strong community that would weather the cold and last over 150 years of change. The residents today now face the hardship of growth in an area that has been discovered again for its beauty and peaceful environment.
